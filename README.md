## SEBI Regulations

The Securities and Exchange Board of India (SEBI) regulates forex trading through specific guidelines that every Indian trader must understand.

### Key Regulatory Points

| Regulation | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Regulator | SEBI (Securities and Exchange Board of India) |
| Authorized exchanges | NSE, BSE, MCX-SX |
| Allowed pairs | INR-based pairs only (USD/INR, EUR/INR, GBP/INR, JPY/INR) |
| Leverage limit | Up to 1:50 on currency futures |
| Minimum lot size | $1,000 equivalent |
| Trading hours | 9:00 AM - 5:00 PM IST (exchange-traded) |
| Overseas forex | Permitted through LRS (Liberalized Remittance Scheme) |

### Liberalized Remittance Scheme (LRS)

Under RBI's LRS framework, Indian residents can remit up to $250,000 per financial year for various purposes including investments abroad. This provides a legal pathway for Indian traders to access international forex brokers.

| LRS Aspect | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Annual limit | $250,000 per person |
| Purpose | Investment, education, travel, medical |
| Tax collection | 20% TCS above INR 7 lakh (adjustable against tax liability) |
| Documentation | PAN card, Aadhaar, bank KYC |
| Processing | Through authorized dealer banks |

## UPI and Payment Methods

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) has revolutionized how Indian traders fund their accounts. Most major international brokers now accept UPI deposits.

### UPI Deposit Process

1. **Register** with a broker that accepts UPI
2. **Verify** your identity with PAN and Aadhaar
3. **Select** UPI as deposit method
4. **Enter** your UPI ID (example@paytm, example@gpay)
5. **Approve** the transaction on your UPI app
6. **Funds** credited within 2-5 minutes

### Payment Method Comparison

## Nifty 50 Trading Strategies

The Nifty 50 index is India's benchmark stock index, and many forex traders also trade Nifty derivatives. For in-depth [Nifty 50 trading strategies and analysis](https://tradingzenith.net/artigos/nifty-50-trading), including technical setups and entry/exit points, see our dedicated strategy guide.

### Popular Nifty 50 Strategies for 2026

#### 1. Gap Trading Strategy
- **Setup:** Identify gaps between previous close and current open
- **Entry:** Trade in the direction of gap fill within first 30 minutes
- **Stop loss:** 0.3% from entry
- **Target:** Gap fill level
- **Best for:** Morning session (9:15-10:00 AM IST)

#### 2. VWAP Strategy
- **Setup:** Use Volume Weighted Average Price as dynamic support/resistance
- **Entry:** Long above VWAP, Short below VWAP
- **Confirmation:** RSI divergence + volume spike
- **Stop loss:** Other side of VWAP
- **Best for:** Trending days

#### 3. Option Selling (Theta Decay)
- **Setup:** Sell OTM options on Wednesday for Thursday expiry
- **Entry:** Sell options at delta 0.15-0.20
- **Risk management:** Defined risk with option spreads
- **Target:** 50-80% premium decay
- **Best for:** Low-volatility, range-bound markets

#### 4. Bank Nifty Scalping
- **Setup:** 5-minute chart with EMA 9/21 crossover
- **Entry:** EMA crossover with volume confirmation
- **Stop loss:** 50 points
- **Target:** 100-150 points
- **Best for:** High-volatility sessions

## Tax Implications

### Forex Trading Tax in India

| Income Type | Tax Rate | Applicable To |
|------------|---------|--------------|
| Speculative income | Slab rate (up to 30%) | Intraday forex trades |
| Non-speculative | Slab rate | Currency futures (carried forward) |
| Short-term capital gains | 15% | Held < 36 months |
| Long-term capital gains | 20% with indexation | Held > 36 months |
| TCS on LRS | 20% above INR 7L | International remittances |

### ITR Filing for Forex Traders

- **ITR-2:** For individuals with capital gains
- **ITR-3:** For individuals with business income (active traders)
- **Audit requirement:** If turnover exceeds INR 10 crore
- **Record keeping:** Maintain trade logs, P&L statements, bank statements

### Key Tax Dates

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| July 31 | ITR filing deadline (non-audit) |
| October 31 | ITR filing deadline (audit cases) |
| March 15 | Last advance tax installment |
| June 15 | First advance tax installment |
